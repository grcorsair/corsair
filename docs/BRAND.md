## The Metaphor

**Ships with 100 cannons but unlocked cargo holds.**

- **Ships** = GRC programs (documented, certified, "compliant")
- **100 cannons** = Pages of policies, frameworks, SOC2/ISO27001 certifications
- **Unlocked cargo hold** = Actual security gaps despite documentation
- **Corsairs** = Authorized raiders who expose these gaps through chaos

**Core Insight:** Most compliance programs are over-documented but under-secured. Corsair raids your infrastructure to prove your controls actually work under adversarial conditions.

## Tone & Voice

### Brand Personality
- **Aggressive but authorized** (sanctioned chaos)
- **Technical depth** (security engineers, not compliance officers)
- **Pirate culture** (raids, plunder, mutiny) mixed with professional security
- **Playful but serious** (retro gaming aesthetic meets real security)

### Writing Style
- Action-oriented commands: `recon`, `raid`, `plunder`, `chart`, `escape`, `parley`
- Pirate terminology aligned with 6 primitives
- Technical precision (not cutesy)
- First-person from Captain's perspective in blog/content

### Command Taxonomy (Pirate-Themed Primitives)
- **RECON** (`corsair recon`) - Scout the territory (reconnaissance)
- **MARK** - Identify targets (implicit in raid planning)
- **RAID** (`corsair raid`) - Execute attack campaigns
- **PLUNDER** (`corsair plunder`) - Extract evidence artifacts
- **CHART** (`corsair chart`) - Map compliance territory
- **ESCAPE** - Clean getaway (automatic rollback)
- **PARLEY** (`corsair parley`) - Negotiate with port authorities (generate reports)

### Examples
- **Documentation:** "The Corsair's Charter" (not "Docs")
- **Community:** "The Crew"
- **Blog:** "Captain's Log"
- **Releases:** "Voyages" (Voyage 1.0, Voyage 2.0)
- **CLI output:** "Hoisting the colors...", "RAID: executing attack...", "PLUNDER: evidence captured...", "ESCAPE: clean getaway..."
